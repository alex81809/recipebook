Ingredients and Instructions

Ingredients

For the 1st bowl
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed (220 g)
  • 1/3 cup melted butter (75 g)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (100 g)
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar (50 g)
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk (120 g)
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract (7 g)
For the 2nd bowl
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour (180 g)
  • 1 tsp baking powder (4 g)
  • 1 tsp baking soda (5 g)
  • 1/2 tsp salt (3 g)
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • mini chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat Oven & Prepare Pan: Preheat your oven to 425°F (218°C) and line a muffin pan with liners.

  2. Combine Dry Ingredients: In one bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon until evenly combined.

  3. Combine Wet Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk the mashed bananas and melted butter until smooth. Add the granulated sugar and brown sugar, mixing until the mixture becomes glossy. Whisk in the eggs, buttermilk, and vanilla extract until fully incorporated.

  4. Combine Wet and Dry: Pour the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients. Gently fold them together until just mostly combined, leaving a few streaks of flour visible. This prevents overmixing.

  5. Add Chocolate Chips: Gently fold in the mini chocolate chips until they are just incorporated.

  6. Rest the Batter: Allow the batter to rest for about 15 minutes. This step helps hydrate the flour, leading to taller, more perfectly domed muffin tops.

  7. Fill Muffin Liners: Fill the muffin liners almost to the top. Sprinkle the tops of the batter with a few extra mini chocolate chips.

  8. Bake: Bake for 5 minutes at 425°F (218°C). Then, reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for another 13–15 minutes. The muffins are ready when a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with only a few moist crumbs.
